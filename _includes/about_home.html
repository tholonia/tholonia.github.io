The Tholonic model aims to integrate consciousness and technology by expanding our understanding of these concepts while remaining firmly on the path of the scientific method.

There have been brilliant thinkers and ideas since Humanity started thinking for itself. While many ideas have evolved and morphed over time, many have stood the test of time, adapting as cultures and perspectives have changed. As no culture or perspective is free of bias, including modern Western culture, it's myopic to disregard all the thinking of our ancestors that does not conform to our current views. It's in our best interest to consider the views and perspectives of our ancestors to be as valid and true for them as ours are to us and to incorporate their views in our efforts to understand the nature of existence. Contrary to many mainstream beliefs today, our understanding does not have a monopoly on truth.

Each item in the list of claims below is fundamental to the tholonic model, and each is a valid hypothesis in that they are based on chain-of-thought reasoning, and none of them has been disproven. Still, of all the claims, there is only one provable fact, and it's the fact that we don't know what energy is. Without energy, there is no existence, yet we don't know where it comes from or why it exists. The Tholonic model attempts to answer those questions. The hypotheses put forth to that end are undoubtedly underdeveloped, and that's why the Tholonic model exists.

The traditional boundaries of consciousness and technology are getting fuzzier by the day, and we are entering a new paradigm that will eventually erase those lines completely. In this new paradigm, as consciousness evolves out of the complexity of technology, consciousness, in all its forms and instances, will be the technology, and technology, as we define that concept today, will be seen as the misplaced efforts of a dying paradigm that could not make sense of the new reality it found itself in.
